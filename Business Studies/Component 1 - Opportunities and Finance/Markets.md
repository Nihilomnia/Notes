## Types of markets

### Local markets
Local markets focus on **one single area** (e.g. it's own city, town, geographic area) and are often small with a limited number of customers and lower levels of sales and profit.

#### Advantages
1. Greater understanding of the culture of their customer base in their community (wants, needs, beliefs and values etc.)
2. Cultural relevance - supporting local events, building community trust, highlighting region-specific issues, campaigns that resonate with area etc.
3. Tighter audience targeting.


#### Challenges 
1. Limited reach: local market will become saturated
2. Dependence on local trends and economies. If the local economy slows or preferences in the area shift rapidly, the business could suffer.
3. Competition from larger corporation (especially price). They potentially mitigate this by prioritizing customer service and emphasis on supporting local.

### Global Markets 

Global markets are **international markets** that are large, often with multinational business selling products to many different countries.

#### Advantages 
1. Much wider reach with greater potential for growth, Expanding globally allows the business to tap into new, potentially lucrative markets.
2. Economies of scale: scaling up operation leads to greater efficiency.
3. Increased brand visibility: globally recognizable brands can add prestige and trust.

#### Challenges 
1. Cultural sensitivity: operating in many different cultural,social,and even political contexts can be tricky to negotiate
2. Logistical complexity: language barriers, regulatory differences, varying consumer behaviors, complex distribution network networks.
3. Fierce competition now up against international giants. Standing out in a crowed global market is difficult.  


### Niche Market 
Where a business targets a smaller segment of a larger market, where customers have specific needs and wants.

#### Advantages 
1. **Reduced competition** due to how specific the market is.
2. **Clear focus** makes it easy to target particular customer
3. Builds up specialist skill and knowledge which leads to **market expertise**.
4. **Higher prices** potentially - customers are prepared to pay for specialty experience
5. **Profit margins** often are higher.
6. Customer tend to be more **loyal**.
#### Disadvantages
1. Lack of **economies of scale**
2. Risk of **over-dependence** on a single product or market.
3. Likely to attract **competition** if successful
4. Vulnerable to markets change changes - all their eggs are in one basket!
### Mass Market 
Where a business sells into the largest part of the market, where there are many similar products on offer.

The business offers products at a large markets, trying to attract the highest number of potential customers rather than particular customers.

Similar goods are produced in large quantities, ignoring market segments and there are attempts to appeal to the entire market.


#### Advantages
1. **Larger customer base**: With a mass markets, businesses have access to a large customer base, which can result in **higher sales and profits**.
2. **Economies of scale**: Mass markets products and services can be produced and distributed at a larger scale, resulting on lower production costs and higher profit margins.
3. **Brand Recognition**: By appealing to a broad audience, mass market products and services can build strong brand recognition and customer loyalty.


#### Disadvantages
1. **More competition**: With a large customer base comes more competition from other business striving for the same customers.
2. **Less personalisation**: Mass market products and services are designed to appeal to a broad audience, which means they **not meet the specific needs and wants** of individual customers.
3. **Lower profits margins**: While mass markets products may sell in larger volumes, the profits margins may lower due to the need to keep prices affordable.

### Trade Markets

Trade marketing involves the sale of goods and services to other business for example wholesalers and retailers, instead of the final consumer.

Producers seek to increase demand for their goods within the supply chain.
 Effective trade marketing makes it far more likely that sales to the final customer would increase.

Trade marketing involves the use of price discounting, promotional offers, point of sale displays and competitions to stimulate demand.

## Market Data 
The challenge for any business is to gain a sufficiently detailed understanding of the fundamentals of a market. Without this insight, it is unlikely that business strategies will prove effective or that objectives will be met.

Gaining this understanding is the task of market analysis.

What are market fundamentals? Glad you asked they are questions such as.
1. How big is the market?
2. How fast is the market growing and what is the market growth potential?
3. What are the key social, economic, political/legal and technological factors that drive change in the market?
4. Who are the existing competitors and what market shares do they have?
5. The extent of branding and customer loyalty in the market?
6. How is the market segmented to meet different customer needs?
7. Customer preferences in terms of hen and where they buy what prices they pay and which methods of promotion are effective?
8. The potential for developing a competitive position in the market - either through a USP or through effective price competition?


The process of analyzing the market should not be considered as one-off and an effective marketing team is constantly searching and updating their market knowledge.

However detailed market analysis is particular important such as:
1. Forecasting sales for new products or investments into new markets.

# Goods

## Characteristics of Goods
1. Tangible: Goods are physical items that touched and seen.
2. Durability: Goods can be classified as durable (long lasting) or non durable goods.
3. Storage: Goods can be stored for future use, allowing for inventory management
4. Transferability: Goods can be transferred from one person to another for ownership changes.


## Types of Goods 

1. Consumer Goods  - These are goods which are **produced for the final consumer**. (Examples includes cars, food and clothes)
2. Capital Goods (Alt. Producer Goods)- These are goods which are **produced for other business** to be able to produce other goods and services. (Examples include computers, robots, and furniture and fixtures)



# Services 

## Characteristics of services
1. Intangible: Services are not physical and cannot be touched or owned.
2. Inseparability: Services are often produced and consumed simultaneously.
3. Variability : The quality of services can vary significantly based on who provides them and standardization is more challenging.
4. Perishabilty: Services cannot be stored for later use.
5. Customer Involvement: Customers often play an active role in the delivery of services, influencing the outcome and experience.
6. Customization: Services can often be tailored to meet the individual's needs and preferences
## Types of Services
1. Personal Services: These are services **provided for individuals**.
2. Commercial Services: These are services that **provide mainly to businesses** such as transport and warehousing, but they may also be available to individuals such as insurance and banking
